Ghada M. Alsebayel

I am a PhD candidate in Human-Computer Interaction at the Khoury College of Computer Sciences, Northeastern University. My research focuses on the design and evaluation of interactive systems for women's and children's health, from menstrual and pregnancy-tracking apps to gamified therapeutics.

About Me

Research Focus

My work sits at the intersection of HCI, digital health, and responsible design. I examine how interactive systems shape trust, agency, and meaningful engagement in intimate health experiences. I have used qualitative analysis, review mining, participatory design, and experimental prototyping. My topics of interest include:


  • Reproductive and maternal health technologies
  • Ethical and privacy-preserving health interface design
  • Gamified and child-centered therapeutic systems
  • User-generated data and opinion mining in health contexts

"An Ad Posing as Medical Advice": User Accounts of Dark UX in FemTech mHealth Apps

We analyze user reviews of menstruation, fertility, pregnancy, and menopause apps to surface dark design patterns: data traps, paywalls at critical moments, and age-related consent gaps.
